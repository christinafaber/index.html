The Mason Jar

An Original Poem by Christina Faber

Here I stand in the aisle of Goodwill
that houses the collection of glassware.
Your bare simplicity attracts my eye, as I’ve been
looking for a vessel to hold my treasures.
I take hold of the firm body you possess and wrap
my lingering fingers around the curvature of
you.
I already imagine how I can fill you
with the things I own;
that would fulfill your purpose.

Turning away from the glasswork and towards the rear of the store,
I raise you to the beams of light that peek through
the dusty floor-to-ceiling window,
searching for potential imperfections in your presence.
It is late afternoon — the bellowing beacon of light in the sky
is visible enough to pool its energy in our direction.

I hold you up higher towards the influence of the sun;
as I came across your place on these shelves, I noticed that
her rays cast a warm and welcoming light
onto your surface.
I wonder; what would occur
if more of her light touched the delicacy of your foundation?

You allow the sun to seep through your transparent glass
and reflect a rainbow onto the concrete floor.
The two of you make a great team;
one provides the energy
and the other interprets the light.

I turn my back towards the sun, still grasping on to
your fragile frame.
Now that your partner is absent, now that the
glow-giver is gone, I assume
you have no power of your own. I suppose
the magnificent sun holds all the strength, as you seem so
fragile, so
delicate, so
dependent.

My shadow covers your body;
your purpose is to be a vessel,
one that holds contents and containment.
With the sun gone and your vase empty,
are you worth anything on your own?

*

You are so much more.
You and the sun complement each other well, but her radiance
does not construct or define you.
Instead, you take what she offers
and turn it into something that is yours.
You are the one who guides her light.

So many will pass you by,
deeming you unnecessary because of your absence of contents
and visible light.
But if they come closer, they will see that your simplicity
is your strength.
Your source of brilliance never came from the shining sun;
she only emphasized what was already there.

I used to think that you were precious in the way
you allowed the light to define your beauty,
to give your emptiness a purpose.
But I now know you were beautiful
before the sun ever graced herself with your touch,
before she ever diffracted her light off your body.

In a way, you create your luminosity.
Not because you are the source of light, but because
you shine despite the absence of it.
You were never truly empty, were you?
Maybe I could learn a thing or two.
